Class 3: Circuit Building Fundamentals 🔧

Welcome Back, Young Engineers! 🚀

Today we're taking our first steps into building real circuits! Don't worry if you can't get all the parts - we'll be using Wokwi, a free online simulator that lets you build and test circuits right from your computer!

Meet Your New Friend: The Breadboard! 🎯

A breadboard is like a Lego board for electronics. It has lots of tiny holes where we can plug in different parts. The magic happens because some of these holes are connected inside the board!

  • The holes in each row are connected (like a train track) 🚂
  • The + and - rails on the sides power your circuit
  • Parts can be easily removed and reused

Resistors 🛑

Controls how much electricity flows - like a water tap!

LEDs 💡

Lights up when electricity flows through it

Switches 🔘

Turns the electricity on and off

Buzzers 🔔

Makes sounds when electricity flows

Let's Build Our First Circuit! 🎮

Physical Components Needed:

  • 1x Breadboard
  • 1x 9V Battery with clip
  • 1x LED (any color)
  • 1x 220Ω Resistor
  • 1x Switch
  • 2x Jumper wires

Using Wokwi Simulator:

  1. Go to wokwi.com
  2. Click "New Project"
  3. Select "Arduino UNO"
  4. Look for the components panel on the right

Step-by-Step Circuit Building 🔨

Physical Build:

  1. Prepare the LED: Notice the longer leg (positive) and shorter leg (negative)
  2. Insert the LED: Long leg in row 10, short leg in row 11
  3. Add the resistor: One end in row 11 with LED's short leg, other end in row 12
  4. Place the switch: In rows 13 and 14
  5. Connect power: Battery clip red wire to LED's long leg, black wire to resistor's other end

Wokwi Build:

  1. Add components: Drag LED, resistor, and switch from the panel
  2. Connect ground: Wire from Arduino GND to breadboard minus rail
  3. Connect power: Wire from Arduino 5V to breadboard plus rail
  4. Build circuit: Follow same pattern as physical build
  5. Click "Start Simulation" to test your circuit

💡 Troubleshooting Tips:

LED not lighting up? Check these common issues:

  • Is the LED in the right direction? (Long leg to positive)
  • Are all connections secure?
  • Is the battery fresh? (For physical build)
  • Are the wires connected to the right pins? (For Wokwi)

Bonus Challenge: Flashing LED! ⚡

Ready to make your LED blink? In Wokwi, we can do this with simple Arduino code:

void setup() {
  pinMode(13, OUTPUT);
}

void loop() {
  digitalWrite(13, HIGH);
  delay(1000);
  digitalWrite(13, LOW);
  delay(1000);
}
